ACORD 25 vs. ACORD 28: Understanding the Key Differences

In the world of insurance and risk management, accurate documentation is crucial for smooth operations and clear communication between various parties. Among these forms, ACORD 25 and ACORD 28 stand out as two commonly used documents. Although they both serve similar purposes—ensuring proper documentation for various types of insurance—they are distinct in their design and application. What is ACORD?

Before we compare ACORD 25 and ACORD 28, it’s important to understand the role of ACORD forms in the insurance industry. ACORD is a non-profit organization that develops and maintains standardized forms and data specifications. These forms are used by insurance companies, agents, brokers, and other stakeholders to facilitate accurate and efficient communication.

The ACORD forms cover a variety of insurance-related topics, including property, casualty, auto, and liability insurance. By using these standardized forms, insurance professionals can ensure that the right information is captured consistently, reducing errors and improving the overall efficiency of the underwriting process.

What is ACORD 25?

ACORD 25, also known as the "Certificate of Liability Insurance," is a commonly used form in the insurance industry. This form is primarily used to provide proof of liability insurance to clients, stakeholders, or third parties. It acts as a certificate or proof that the named insured has liability insurance coverage for specific policies, typically in the context of business operations.

Key Elements of ACORD 25:

  • Purpose: The primary purpose of the ACORD 25 is to verify that the insured party has valid liability insurance coverage in place. It is often requested by businesses or contractors as part of due diligence when entering into agreements or partnerships.
  • Contents: The form includes essential details about the insurance policy, including the insurer’s name, policy number, limits of coverage, and effective dates. It also specifies the type of liability coverage, such as general liability, auto liability, or professional liability, and any endorsements or special provisions that may apply to the policy.
  • Common Use: ACORD 25 is commonly used by businesses that need to demonstrate their insurance coverage to clients or regulatory agencies. For example, contractors often provide this form to property owners to prove that they are covered in case of accidents or damages during a project.

What is ACORD 28?

ACORD 28, also known as the "Certificate of Property Insurance," is another widely used form in the insurance industry. As the name suggests, it serves as a certificate of insurance for property coverage, specifically verifying that a property is insured against potential risks, such as damage, theft, or loss.

Key Features of ACORD 28:

  • Purpose: The ACORD 28 form’s main purpose is to provide proof that a property is insured. This can include commercial properties, residential buildings, or even specific assets like equipment or inventory. It is commonly requested when property owners or managers need to show proof of insurance coverage for their real estate or other valuable assets.
  • Contents: Similar to ACORD 25, the ACORD 28 includes important details such as the insurer's name, policy number, coverage limits, and effective dates. It also specifies the type of property coverage, such as fire, theft, vandalism, or natural disaster, and lists any exclusions or endorsements.
  • Common Use: ACORD 28 is frequently used in situations where property owners, landlords, or mortgage lenders need proof that a property is properly insured. This could include commercial leases, real estate transactions, or when obtaining financing for a property.

Key Differences Between ACORD 25 and ACORD 28

While both ACORD 25 and ACORD 28 are essential forms used in the insurance industry, they differ in several important ways. Let’s explore the key differences:

  • Type of Coverage: ACORD 25 provides proof of liability insurance coverage, while ACORD 28 provides proof of property insurance coverage.
  • Application: ACORD 25 is typically used by businesses or contractors to show liability coverage, whereas ACORD 28 is used by property owners, landlords, and real estate professionals to show property insurance.
  • Document Focus: ACORD 25 focuses on liability insurance details, while ACORD 28 focuses on property insurance details.
  • Industry Usage: ACORD 25 is common in construction, transportation, and business sectors, while ACORD 28 is used primarily in property management and real estate transactions.

When Should You Use ACORD 25 vs. ACORD 28?

Understanding when to use ACORD 25 or ACORD 28 depends on the specific needs of the situation. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Use ACORD 25 when you need to provide proof of liability insurance for a business or individual.
  • Use ACORD 28 when you need to provide proof that a property is insured against risks like fire, theft, or other potential damages.
